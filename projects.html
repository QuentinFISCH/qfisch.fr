KubeKing

Brief Presentation Présentation rapide

KubeKing is a 2D platformer game made with Unity. While creating a 3D game for our second semester project (MindBlow), my friend Charles and I decided to learn how to make a 2D game. It was at first just for fun, but we found a lot of potiential on this little game. In fact, this game is very fun to play! It is a platformer game containing 10 levels: 8 are free, the other two are available on an in-game store where you can also buy new characters and items. For each level, you need to find 3 pieces to be able to complete the challenge. The faster you complete a level, the more pieces you earn. However, you will quickly understand that the mecanisms introduced in the game make some levels very hard to finsih within a good time. But if you are determinated, try and retry the levels to collect enough pieces to buy shops' items. Find more information about the game on Bitarrays' website! MindBlow

Brief Presentation Présentation rapide

During the second semester of my first year at EPITA, we needed to create a project in either OCaml or C#. My friends and I formed a group of 4 students. We wanted to create a game, which is MindBlow. The game contains two different modes: solo and cooperation. The solo mode is composed of 8 uniques levels, each more challenging than the previous ones. Find your way out of a giant medieval castle while resolving mathematics, logic or visualisation based enigmas.
The coop mode is a bit shorter, containing 4 levels. However, if you manage to get out of the prison, you and your mate are communication gods. Test you friendship for free, or discover KubeKing!

Ping-pong balls thrower Lanceur de balles de ping-pong

Description

During my last two highschool years, I studied Engineering Sciences. It was one of my favorite courses, therefore, I was very dedicated to each projects we could possibly do. The final highschool's year, we had to create a project, involving mechanics and electronics. I was in love with the idea, and I grouped up with two of my friends who where determinated too. Our teacher gave us a project idea, which was to improve a ping-pong balls thrower from the public market. This public robot was able to shoot balls with only one effect, and couldn't automaticaly change the throwing angle to shoot on different table's parts. So, we thought it was a good idea to try automate it a bit and add some more features.
We decided to add several shooting effects, such as "normal", "lift" and "shop". Furthermore, we wanted to be able to throw balls on either the forehand, the backhand or the body of the player. We also wanted to included a moving mechanic to the robot, even if it had not a very important impact on a ball throw: we put the robot on a rail, permitting it to move to the left or the right of the table. Finally, we added a smash system, where the thrower was enhanced, and the head of the robot looking down.
Since we did not have time to create a website for this project, a complete presentation of the part I can present can be seen throught the different windows.

This part represents the calculs regarding the strip on which the carriage moves. We can see that by having the angular rotation w2, we can obtain the linear speed of the strip. Then, we take the mass of the robot and associated it with the friction coefficient to have the tangential effort. Not all these values are usefull, but some of them are really important to determinate the needed composants' caracteristics. For example, if we add the pulley radius to the previous values, we can determinate the torque needed to move the carriage. Cette partie représente les calculs concernant la bande sur laquelle se déplace le chariot. Nous pouvons voir qu'en ayant la rotation angulaire w2, nous pouvons obtenir la vitesse linéaire de la bande. Ensuite, on prend la masse du robot et on l'associe au coefficient de frottement pour avoir l'effort tangentiel. Toutes ces valeurs ne sont pas utiles, mais certaines sont vraiment importantes pour déterminer les caractéristiques des composants nécessaires. Par exemple, si nous ajoutons le rayon de la poulie aux valeurs précédentes, nous pouvons déterminer le couple nécessaire pour déplacer le le chariot.

Here is the graph corresponding to the motor used to move the robot on the rail. We take the torque from the motor on use several constants to determinate the angular rotation, to calculate later the voltage to apply to the motor, in order to move the robot for 1.5m in 2secs. Voici le graphique correspondant au moteur utilisé pour déplacer le robot sur le rail. Nous prenons le couple du moteur en utilisant plusieurs constantes pour déterminer la rotation angulaire, afin de calculer plus tard la tension à appliquer au moteur, permettant de déplacer le robot sur 1,5 m en 2 secondes.
